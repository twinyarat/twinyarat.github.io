The Cholesky Decomposition

\(\)

The Cholesky decomposition of a symmetric, positive definite matrix \(A\) is a product of a lower triangular matrix \(L^T\) and its transpose \(L\):

\[A = L^TL\]

To derive the decomposition proceduere, let \(A\) be a symmetric, positive definite \(n \times n\) matrix partitioned into the following block form:

\[\begin{align*} \begin{bmatrix} A_{11} & A_{1,2:n} \\ A_{1,2:n}^T & A_{2:n, 2:n} \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{bmatrix} L_{11} & 0 \\ L^T_{1,2:n} & L^T_{2:n,2:n} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} L_{11} & L_{1,2:n} \\ 0 & L_{2:n,2:n} \end{bmatrix} \\ &= \begin{bmatrix} L_{11}^2 & L_{11}L_{1,2:n} \\ L^T_{1,2:n}L_{11} & L^T_{1,2:n}L_{1,2:n} + L^T_{2:n,2:n}L_{2:n,2:n} \end{bmatrix} \end{align*}\]

The upper left scalar gives us the first constraint:

\[A_{11} = L^2_{11} \Rightarrow L_{11} = \sqrt{A_{11}} \tag{eq.1}\]

The upper right block gives us the second constraint:

\[A_{1,2:n} = L_{11}L_{1,2:n} \Rightarrow L_{1,2:n} = (\frac{1}{L_{11}})A_{1,2:n} \tag{eq.2}\]

And lastly, the lower right block gives us:

\[A_{2:n,2:n} - L^T_{1,2:n}L_{1,2:n}= L_{2:n,2:n}^TL_{2:n,2:n} \tag{eq.3}\]

,whose left hand side is also symmetric, positive definite. The algorithm continues by recusively solving for the Cholesky factor \(L^T_{2:n,2:n}\) of the smaller subproblem. To see that the LHS of eq.3 is indeed symmetric, positive definite, proceed from the fact that \(A \succ 0\):

\[\forall \begin{pmatrix} y \\ x \end{pmatrix} \neq \vec{0}, \begin{pmatrix} y \\ x \end{pmatrix}^T A \begin{pmatrix} y \\ x \end{pmatrix} > 0\] \[\begin{align*} \Rightarrow \qquad& \begin{pmatrix} y \\ x \end{pmatrix}^T \begin{bmatrix} A_{11} & A_{1,2:n} \\ A_{1,2:n}^T & A_{2:n, 2:n} \end{bmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} y \\ x \end{pmatrix} > 0 \\ \\ \Rightarrow \qquad & y^T A_{11}y + y^TA^T_{2:n,1}x + x^TA_{2:n,1}y + x^TA_{2:n,2:n}x > 0 \\ \\ &\text{Define } y := -\frac{A^T_{2:n,1}x}{A_{11}} \\ \\ \Rightarrow \qquad& \frac{-1}{A_{11}}x^TA_{2:n,1}A^T_{2:n,1}x + x^TA_{2:n,2:n}x > 0 \\ \\ \Rightarrow \qquad& x^T \bigg\{ \frac{-1}{A_{11}}A_{2:n,1}A^T_{2:n,1} + A_{2:n,2:n} \bigg\} x > 0 \end{align*}\]

(Vandenberghe, 2019)
